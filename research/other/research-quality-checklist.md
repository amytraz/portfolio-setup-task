# Research Quality Checklist

## Assignment Fit
- One topic chosen: AI-powered SEO content production.
- 10 experts documented in `research/sources.md`.
- LinkedIn/blog post notes collected for all 10 experts.
- YouTube transcript/caption artifacts collected for 4 experts and 8 videos.
- Additional collection notes stored in `research/other/`.
- README explains what was collected and why the experts were chosen.

## Source Quality
- Experts are practitioners, founders, consultants, operators, or tool builders.
- The set includes multiple viewpoints: technical SEO, AEO/GEO, semantic SEO, content optimization, quality recovery, distribution, and operational content production.
- The collection avoids relying on only generic first-page Google results.

## Repository Structure
```text
research/
  sources.md
  linkedin-posts/
  youtube-transcripts/
    raw/
  other/
```

## Playbook Readiness
The collected material is strong enough to support a later playbook because it contains repeatable guidance on:

- Choosing high-intent topics and content formats.
- Structuring content for LLM extraction and AI citations.
- Building topical authority and entity clarity.
- Avoiding low-quality scaled AI content.
- Combining content production with distribution.
- Measuring visibility through AI mentions, citations, branded presence, and high-intent page performance.

## Remaining Caveats
- LinkedIn post content can change or become gated by login requirements.
- YouTube auto-captions may contain transcription errors.
- The transcript notes summarize research insights; raw caption files are stored separately for traceability.
