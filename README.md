# AI-Powered SEO Content Production Research

## Topic
AI-powered SEO content production

This repository collects high-signal practitioner content about how SEO and content teams are adapting to AI search, answer engines, generative engine optimization, and scalable AI-assisted content workflows.

## What I Collected
- 10 expert source profiles in `research/sources.md`
- 32 LinkedIn/blog post notes in `research/linkedin-posts/`
- 8 YouTube video transcript research notes in `research/youtube-transcripts/`
- 15 raw YouTube caption artifacts in `research/youtube-transcripts/raw/`
- Collection and verification notes in `research/other/`

## Why These Experts
I chose practitioners who actively build, advise, or operate SEO/content systems, not generic commentators. The list balances:

- Search quality and E-E-A-T expertise: Lily Ray, Aleyda Solis, Mike King
- AEO/GEO and AI-search visibility specialists: Connor Kimball, Jason Barnard, Bernard Huang, Nathan Gotch
- Semantic SEO and topical authority: Koray Tugberk Gubur
- Distribution-led content strategy for B2B: Ross Simmonds
- Broad operational SEO/content production: Neil Patel

Together, the sources cover the playbook-level questions this research needs to support later: what to create, how to structure it, how to make it machine-readable, how to distribute it, and how to measure visibility when traffic and rankings are no longer enough.

## Collection Method
YouTube metadata and captions were verified with `yt-dlp` as a free transcript/caption extraction method. LinkedIn posts were collected from public post URLs and summarized into consistent research notes with dates, links, summaries, and key insights.

See `research/other/collection-method.md` for the exact commands, video manifest, and limitations.

## Quality Bar
I prioritized a smaller set of high-signal sources over volume. Each expert was selected because their content can support a real future playbook for AI-powered SEO content production, especially around:

- AI-search visibility and answer-engine optimization
- High-intent content and topical authority
- Structured content, schema, and machine-readable pages
- Human expertise and brand trust as defenses against generic AI content
- Distribution across LinkedIn, YouTube, Reddit, communities, and owned media

## Commit History
The work was committed progressively across the research process instead of as one final dump. The Git history includes separate commits for folder setup, expert source selection, LinkedIn post collection, expert replacement/refinement, and YouTube transcript research.
